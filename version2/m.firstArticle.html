Can Fortnite Pressure Sony to Finally Agree to Cross-Play?

published by: Ethan Prytherch

June 13th, 2018 | 11:30am

One year ago at E3 2017, Mojang announced at Microsoft’s press conference that they would be bringing two new updates to the game. One of the updates would be brand new 4K graphics that would give the game a significant visual upgrade. While there have been mods for the Java version on PC that have achieved the same results, this would be bringing the updated visuals to the entire Minecraft community. The bigger update of the two would be the Better Together Update, promising to unite the console ecosystem through cross-play. A year has passed and while we are still waiting for the Better Together Update to release on Switch, it is available now across Xbox, PC, VR, and Mobile, the promise of being able to take your world with you across any device was an exciting prospect. While the announcement pertained to Minecraft the big question still lingered, could cross-play between all the major consoles finally be upon us?

Minecraft can be considered one of the biggest games in the world. Seeing as Microsoft paid $2.5 Billion for Minecraft I’d say they would agree, and I saw the announcement as an opportunity for cross-play to finally break the barrier between consoles and to allow everyone to play together. However there was one noticeable system missing from the announcement video, and that system just so happened to be the same one leading this current console generation in sales. PlayStation was nowhere to be found, and many including myself had hoped this was either a temporary issue or that maybe Sony hadn’t had a chance to finalize the details yet and it would just be a matter of time. It took almost no time for an answer. As the community and press started to ask questions, it become apparent that this was not a mistake but a strategic move by Sony.

On June 13th 2017, two days after the initial announcement at Microsoft’s E3 conference, Eurogamer sat down with Sony’s Jim Ryan, head of global marketing and sales, to ask why they would be holding out from cross-play with Minecraft. While he seemed to express how much the community is interested in this, the bottom line was that Sony was not interested in participating at the time. In one statement he expressed concern for their younger demographic, ͞Exposing what in many cases are children to external influences we have no ability to manage or look after, its something we have to think about very carefully.͟ While this answer attempts to evoke a sentiment that they are doing what is best for their player base, the community has made it very clear however that this is not what they want.

In the last year we have seen no movement from Sony on this issue and I was personally hoping that this current E3 would see Sony come to stage and announce they were doing what is best for the players by joining in on cross-play. While Minecraft may have been the biggest game thus far to attempt to get Sony on board, they are not the only ones trying to make this happen. Psyonix has brought cross-play to consoles with their mega hit Rocket League, where you can currently play between Switch, Xbox, and PC.

This E3 has brought us what I think is the biggest opportunity yet for cross-play to finally happen, Epic Games’ Fortnite. Over the past year Fortnite’s Battle Royale mode has reached unprecedented levels of popularity and now finds itself on every platform imaginable. This is largely because it is free to play allowing everyone to at least try the game. However Epic’s commitment to the community through updates and engagement with fans across social media has helped lead to its Meteoric rise. While not everyone may love Fortnite and the recent explosion of Battle Royale games can be attributed to a combination of both Fortnite and PubG, it’s hard to argue against its success with the game making $296 million in April and on pace for $3.5 billion for all of 2018. It makes perfect sense then that with all the success Fortnite has found it was only a matter of time until it partnered with Nintendo and found its way to the Switch.

Nintendo held its annual E3 Direct yesterday and with it announced that Fortnite would be coming to Switch and it would be available within the hour. Despite being leaked in the weeks prior to E3 this was still an exciting announcement for both the Fortnite and Switch community as now you would be able to take the game with you and continue to level up your Battle Pass even away from home. The prospect of the biggest game on one of the hottest consoles should have been an easy Home Run. It wasn’t long after 10am that as players started logging into the game with their accounts they were met with an unsightly screen. If you had at any point logged into your Epic Games account on a PS4 then you would not be able to use that account on the Switch. For PS4 players this meant either starting from scratch or purchasing an additional Battle Pass that would have separate progression and fragment your items across consoles. For anyone who used their account on IOS, Xbox, or PC, they were easily able to sync up their accounts and jump right into a match with everything they had earned and purchased prior. The reaction from the PS4 community was understandably outrage.

The reaction over the past day has quickly far exceeded anything we saw over the Minecraft announcement. With Fortnite players having invested large amounts of time and money into the game and the thought of having to leave this all behind is one they will not stand for. One Reddit user claimed that after logging over 700 hours on PC and only logging in once on PS4, he was now locked out from linking his Epic account to Switch. A reddit post on the Fortnite Battle Royale subreddit, as of the time of writing this, gained almost 31 thousand upvotes and over 2 thousand comments of players expressing their frustration with Sony’s lack of cooperation with allowing cross-play. It seems that Sony is not going to be let off the hook this time around and it may be that it takes the biggest game in the world right now to finally force Sony’s hand. It will be interesting to see in the coming days, weeks, and months if the Fortnite community is able to use its numbers to get Sony’s attention and show them that their current strategy is not what the players want or support. The fact that they are now the only ones not supporting cross-play is only going to continue to upset their supporters and degrade their image.

If Fortnite is finally able to finally break through and make this change happen, it will only be better for the entire gaming community. Sony’s stubbornness and lack of transparency on this issue may turn out to hurt them in the long run if they are unable to keep pace with the ever-evolving industry. Time will tell if this ends up having any lasting impact on their image or if it will take until the next generation of consoles and a potential lead by Microsoft for Sony to finally agree, but with Fortnite currently leading the charge it seems they have the best chance of anyone.
